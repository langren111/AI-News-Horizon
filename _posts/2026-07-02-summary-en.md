## [LLMs Fail Physics Reasoning in Unfamiliar Worlds](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00276) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Researchers introduced a four-stage diagnostic to test LLMs' physics reasoning in counterfactual and historical frameworks, finding that Claude Opus 4.7, GPT-5.5, and Gemini 3.1 Pro achieved composite PASS rates of only 6/15, 6/15, and 0/15 across three parallel physics worlds. This work addresses a critical gap in LLM evaluation by moving beyond answer accuracy to assess genuine reasoning, revealing that current models struggle to adapt to unfamiliar physics rules, which has implications for AI safety and reliability in scientific applications. The diagnostic includes locked pre-registrations, fresh sessions between stages, dual-LLM judging, and a human-audit pathway, and it uncovered a qualitative-versus-quantitative asymmetry: models rarely predict the wrong direction of change but frequently compute wrong ratios by reverting to standard physics.

rss · ArXiv CS.AI · Jul 2, 04:00

**Background**: Current LLM physics benchmarks typically score by answer accuracy, which cannot distinguish genuine reasoning from recall of familiar patterns. This diagnostic tests reasoning through induction, formulation, prediction, and review in three parallel physics worlds: a counterfactual F=mv world, Aristotelian mechanics, and a Decay World with modified decay laws.

## [Hallucinated Citations Survive Peer Review at Top AI Conferences](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00738) ⭐️ 9.0/10

A new study introduces RefChecker, a verification pipeline, and finds that hallucinated citations—non-existent or mismatched references—appear in peer-reviewed papers from top venues like NeurIPS, ICML, ICLR, and USENIX Security, with roughly one in twenty NeurIPS 2025 papers containing at least two likely hallucinated references. This work reveals that peer review alone cannot reliably enforce citation integrity, threatening scientific trust and AI safety, as LLM-generated hallucinations can enter the archival record. The open-source RefChecker tool offers a scalable, low-cost solution ($0.04 per paper) for pre-publication verification. RefChecker uses a conservative definition of citation hallucination, focusing only on identity-level failures like non-existent works and substantial author-list mismatches, excluding ordinary bibliographic drift. The pipeline resolves bibliography entries against multiple sources and escalates unresolved cases to web-search re-verification.

rss · ArXiv CS.AI · Jul 2, 04:00

**Background**: Large language models (LLMs) can generate plausible-sounding but fabricated citations, a phenomenon known as citation hallucination. This is a subset of LLM hallucination, where models invent facts instead of admitting uncertainty. Previous work has documented such hallucinations in legal and academic contexts, but this study is the first to systematically measure their prevalence in peer-reviewed conference proceedings.

However, empirical evidence shows preferences are dynamic and shaped by interaction, especially with adaptive technologies. Constructive Alignment addresses this gap by proposing a paradigm that governs how AI influences preference evolution, ensuring value trajectories remain coherent, reflectively endorsed, and resistant to manipulation.

## [IPFS Content Publishing Made 10x Faster via Async RPCs](https://probelab.io/blog/optimistic-provide/) ⭐️ 6.0/10

ProbeLab announced a 10x speedup in IPFS content publishing by making most PUT RPCs asynchronous, returning control to the user after most operations succeed while continuing the rest in the background. This improvement significantly reduces the perceived latency for IPFS content publishers, making the system more practical for real-time or interactive use cases, though it does not reduce total work. The optimization defers a subset of PUT RPCs to background execution, so the reported speedup is in user-perceived time, not total processing time. The approach is incremental and specific to IPFS's providing process.

**Background**: IPFS (InterPlanetary File System) is a decentralized protocol for storing and sharing files. Content publishing in IPFS involves announcing content to the network via PUT RPCs to a Distributed Hash Table (DHT), which can be slow due to network latency and confirmation waits.
